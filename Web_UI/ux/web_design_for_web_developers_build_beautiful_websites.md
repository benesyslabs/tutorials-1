### Beautiful Typography ###
1. Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language readable and beautiful
	1. Essential part of a design
		1. Most of the website will be text-content
	2. Good typography makes every design professional
2. Rules
	1. **Use a font-size between 15 and 25 pixels** for body text
		1. Depends on typeface
		2. Depends on the amount of text
		3. Depends on how much we want the text to stand out
	2. **Use (Really) Big font sizes for headlines** and titles
		1. Headline - 60px
			1. 90px
				1. Reduce the font-weight as the font-size increases
					1. Ensures it doesn't steal too much attention from the rest of the content
					2. Ensures the text doesn't look bulky, but modern and elegant
		2. Subtitle - 32px
	3. **Use line spacing between 120% and 150%** of the font-size
		1. Vertical spacing between lines
			1. Makes text easier to read
				1. Too much - Eyes may have a difficult time processing the text
	4. **45 to 90 characters per line**
		1. Easier to read (2 - 3 lowercase alphabet)
		2. Too little
			1. Looks weird unless there are multiple columns (especially if too much text)
		3. Too long
			1. Eyes find it difficult to scan
	5. **Use good fonts**
		1. Sans-serif
			1. More neutral
			2. Clean
			3. Simple
			4. Modern websites
			5. Good examples: Google fonts (Web-fonts - we can included directly without downloading). We can choose any of them
				1. **Open Sans**
				2. **Lato**
				3. **Raleway**
				4. **Monsterrat**
				5. **PT Sans**
		2. Serif
			1. Traditional purposes
			2. Storytelling
			3. Long reading
				1. We can mix serif and sans serif
			4. Good examples: Google fonts
				1. **Cardo**
				2. **Merriweather**
				3. **PT Serif**
			5. When to use?
				1. If too much text to show
					1. Blog website
					2. To have a more traditional look
		3. How to choose a font?
			1. Choose a font which reflects the look and feel you want for your website
				1. Should engage audience
			2. Decide: sans-serif or serif typeface?
			3. Use a good font
			4. Use only that one typeface
				1. Especially for beginners
					1. If we want to use more than one, check the e-book (shows how to combine)

### The Meaning of Colors in Web Design ###
1. Picking a color means picking the right color to get desired response from audience
2. Color makes a difference because there are psychological effects behind each color
3. Colors:

		Red is a great color to use when power, passion, strength and excitement want to be transmitted. Brighter tones are more energetic and darker shades are more powerful and elegant.
		
		Orange draws attention without being as overpowering as red. It means cheerfulness and creativity. Orange can be associated with friendliness, confidence, and courage.
		
		Yellow is energetic and gives the feeling of happiness and liveliness. Also, it associates with curiosity, intelligence, brightness, etc.
		
		Green is the color of harmony, nature, life and health. Also, it is often associated with money. In design, green can have a balancing and harmonizing effect.
		
		Blue means patience, peace, trustworthiness, and stability. It is one of the most beloved colors, especially by men. It is associated with professionalism, trust and honor. That's actually why the biggest social networks use blue.
		
		Purple is traditionally associated with power, nobility and wealth. In your design, purple can give a sense of wisdom, royalty, nobility, luxury, and mystery.
		
		Pink expresses romance, passivity, care, peace, affection, etc.
		
		Brown is the color of relaxation and confidence. Brown means earthiness, nature, durability, comfort, and reliability.

### Working with Images ###
1. Tips
	1. **Using large background images makes website more attractive and help in user engagement**
		1. **Image heavy websites convert better**
			1. We need to learn how to work with images
				1. How to put text over images
					1. Techniques
2. Techniques:
	1. **Put text directly on the image**
		1. Problem: Works only when image is dark and text is white
			1. There wont be much contrast otherwise
			2. Solution:
				1. **Overlay the image with a color**
	2. **Overlay the image with a color**
		1. Black is common
		2. Other colors are fine
		3. Use color gradients
	3. **Put your text in a box**
		1. Box should be opaque (some transparency)
		2. Can use black or other color as per website
	4. **Blur the image**
		1. Blur the whole image or use out of focus area of a photo as blur
			1. Ensure that the text stays on blurred image part on all screen resolutions
	5. **The floor fade**
		1. Images subtly fades to black at the bottom with white text over it
			1. Increases readability
				1. Good for text at the bottom of the page

### Spacing and Layout ###
1. Every design uses whitespace well
	1. To make website that looks designed
2. Whitespace - space where there is nothing
	1. Essential to construct a clean and simple look and feel
	2. Shows room to breethe
3. Guidelines
	1. **Use whitespace**
		1. Put whitespace between your elements
		2. Put whitespace between your groups of elements
		3. Put whitespace between your website's sections
		4. But don't exaggerate
			1. If elements are spaced too much, they may lose the relationship with one another
	2. **Define hierarchy**
		1. Whitespace describes invisible relationships between the elements of your website
			1. It communicates how pieces of information relate to one another (very important)
				1. Visual hierarchy (organization of content)
		2. Hierachy gives websites order
			1. We need to get it right
				1. Guidelines:
					1. **Define where you want your audience to look first**
					2. **Establish a flow that corresponds to your content's message**
						1. Hierarchy guides the user from one element to the next
					3. Use whitespace to build that flow

### Introduction to User Experience ###
1. **User experience** is the presentation of a product (website/mobile app), how it looks and feels
	1. Design: **User experience** is the overall experience the user has with a product
		1. UI + the whole picture of the product
			1. "It's not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works" - Steve Jobs
				1. Difference between designing a **presentation** vs designing the **experience**
					1. Presentation: bottle is beautiful but doesn't solve the user's need to get the ketchup out of the bottle quickly
					2. Experience: Pleasant and beautiful and sqeezable bottle
2. User experience:
	1. UX designer needs to align:
		1. User's goals
		2. Business' goals
	2. Business must benefit when the user reaches their goal
		1. Example: Free e-book in exchange of email address
			1. User's goal: To learn about a specific subject from the book
			2. Business goal: To get email address of customers to sell them something later
	3. A design must do the best possible job to achieve both goals (user's and business')

### Quiz 2: Web Design Quiz 2 ###
### 8 Super Effective Ways to Improve Your Website's Conversion ###
1. To sell something (course, ebook, subscription)
	1. To improve website conversion rates
2. Guidelines
	1. **Build trust with your future customer**
		1. Offer visitors free gift to build trust
			1. Don't sell anything right away
				1. Example: Ebook
					1. If user likes the book, he is likely to buy anything after that
	2. **Repeat your primary action**
		1. Have a strong CTA button
			1. Where we want users to click
				1. repeat it as many times as it looks good in different places (don't spam and frustrate the user)
					1. Top right
					2. Middle
					3. Bottom nav bar
	3. **Grab your user's attention**
		1. Assume a sign up form
			1. Use a popup box if possible
				1. email address
				2. They can be annoying
					1. They convert well
						1. They do work
	4. **Tell your user the benefit** of clicking the CTA button
		1. Extremely important
			1. Example: TRY IT FOR FREE (verb and benefit the user will get)
	5. **Don't ask for too much information** than we need
		1. User doesn't want to go through too many fields to achieve something
			1. Fewer fields can convert better
	6. **Use social proof**
		1. Great persuation tactic that can increase conversion rates
			1. Types:
				1. Customer testimonials (if any)
				2. Other well known companies that use our product/service
					1. Powerful because customers are more likely to trust others than to trust us
						1. Use it whenever we can
	7. **Use urgency** or scarcity
		1. Used to make users act quickly
			1. Used to make people act now rather than later
				1. Examples:
					1. Limited time remaining!
		2. Words to use:
			1. Now
			2. Instantly
			3. Hurry
			4. Immediately
			5. Remaining
	8. **User scarcity**
		1. Feeling that a product or service is in short supply and it is about to run out
			1. When there is less of anything we tend to value it more
				1. Makes user act now

## Section 3: Course Summary ##
### Wrapping up What We've Learned ###
Let's quickly summarize the main content of the course:

* Most of your content will be text, so beautiful typography is a key element to a good looking website.
* Images are getting more and more important in webdesign, so choosing great images and putting text on them is an essential part of your work.
* Icons are also a good way of setting a friendly tone for your website, but use them carefully.
* The adequate use of whitespace makes a website look professionally designed, so use it a lot, but in the correct way.
* Build a layout by defining the visual hierarchy of your content. Whitespace is also important for this.
* Your website should be designed in a way that ensures that both the user and the owner of the website achieve their goals. This is the user experience.
* It is very important that you get inspired by studying well-designed websites from other designers.
